Understanding the Concept of Remote Online Notary

A Remote Online Notary (RON) is a state-authorized notary public who performs notarizations online using secure audio-visual technology. Unlike traditional notary services, which require in-person interactions, remote online notaries allow individuals to complete the process from the comfort of their homes or offices. How Does a Remote Online Notary Work?

Remote online notarization involves a streamlined process that ensures security and compliance with legal standards. Here’s how it works:

  • Scheduling the Appointment: Clients initiate the process by scheduling an online session with a Remote Online Notary.
  • Identity Verification: The notary verifies the signer’s identity using government-issued IDs and advanced technologies such as credential analysis and knowledge-based authentication (KBA).
  • Document Submission: The document to be notarized is uploaded to the notary’s secure platform.
  • Audio-Visual Session: The signer and notary connect through a live audio-visual platform.
  • Electronic Signature and Notary Seal: The signer applies their electronic signature, and the notary affixes their official digital seal.
  • Receiving the Notarized Document: Once the process is complete, the notarized document is securely delivered to the signer.

The Difference Between Mobile Notary Services and Remote Online Notary

While both mobile notary services and remote online notaries provide convenient alternatives to traditional notarization, there is a key difference between the two. A mobile notary service involves a notary traveling to a client’s location to perform notarizations in person. In contrast, a Remote Online Notary conducts the entire process digitally, eliminating the need for physical meetings.
